🏥 NHS Resource Utilisation & Missed Appointments

Diagnostic Analytics Project • Python • NHS + Twitter Data

📌 Overview

This project explores appointment data from the NHS in England to evaluate system utilisation, missed appointments, and public sentiment from August 2021 to June 2022. Combining structured NHS data with public Twitter posts, the goal was to uncover bottlenecks, inform smarter policy, and highlight public concerns in real time.

⚠️ Challenge

The NHS faces immense pressure from rising demand, staff shortages, and missed appointments. Stakeholders needed to understand:

  • Are existing NHS resources overstretched?
  • What patterns exist in missed appointments?
  • Can sentiment from the public help shape interventions?

🛠️ Tools Used

Languages & Libraries: Python, Pandas, Seaborn, Matplotlib, Scikit-learn, VADER
Environment: Jupyter Notebook, GitHub, HTML export

✅ Actions Taken

  • Cleaned and merged 3 NHS datasets (appointments, durations, categories)
  • Analysed capacity vs planning thresholds across time and region
  • Modelled DNA (Did Not Attend) risk using logistic regression
  • Used VADER for Twitter sentiment analysis and hashtag trends

📊 Results

  • Systems ran at >110% capacity most months — October peaked at 120%
  • 1.5M missed appointments in October alone, driven by short-notice & remote bookings
  • South East London had lowest access per capita; Cornwall the highest
  • Twitter hashtags revealed spikes in #job, #digitalhealth, #nhscrisis

🧠 Insights

  • Utilisation: Chronic overcapacity is the norm, not the exception
  • Access: Dense urban ICBs are underserved compared to rural ones
  • DNA risk: Short notice and telephone appointments are most vulnerable
  • Public mood: Social media offers real-time sentiment monitoring

💡 Recommendations

  • 🔔 Send smart reminders for short-notice appointments
  • 👩‍⚕️ Use dynamic rosters and locum pools during known demand peaks
  • 📍 Prioritise funding for high-demand ICBs with low per capita access
  • 📊 Improve data quality (e.g., age, comorbidities) for better prediction
  • 📡 Track Twitter sentiment weekly to detect trust breakdowns early
